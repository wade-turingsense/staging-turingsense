Categories of Personally Identifiable Information that TuringSense Collects

TuringSense may collect information that you provide to TuringSense in connection with your use of the Service when you: (A) register your account with TuringSense as a user of the Service (“Customers”); (B) make changes to your user profile information; (C) send email messages, forms, or other information to TuringSense or other users using the Service; (D) install, use or otherwise interact with the Service. This information may include your first and last name, address, email address, telephone number, profile photo, and any additional information you provide to TuringSense during the account registration process or via the Service.

When you use our products, you will be interacting with our fitness tracking sensors. These sensors will transmit information to your mobile device via our mobile application and will collect information about your physical movements and the positioning of your body. The Service may use this information to derive additional information, such as the type and frequency of activities that you may be performing while using the Service.

TuringSense may also collect certain technical information about your use of the Service. This technical information may include information about your device(s), browser type and version, geo-location, computer, computer connection, statistics on page views, traffic to and from the Service, Wi-Fi connection, internet protocol (“IP”) address, and standard web log.

When you register to use the Service, we require you to provide us with your contact e-mail and name (“Required Information”). You must provide this information in order to access and use the Service. If you do not wish to provide this information, please do not access or use the Service.

In order to facilitate our analysis of your physical performance, you may, but are not required to, provide us information about yourself that will help you enjoy the Service (“Optional Information”). This information includes your height, gender, age, handedness, and skill level. Although you will not need to provide this information to us in order to access and use the Service, providing us this information will improve your experience with the Service.

Although we use third party service providers to process payments, when purchasing access to the Service, we may require you to provide our payment processing service providers with financial and billing information, such as billing name and address and your credit card number (“Financial Information”). For more information, please refer to the section in this Privacy Policy entitled “Categories of Third-Party Persons or Entities With Whom TuringSense May Share Your Personally Identifiable Information.” Required Contact Information, Financial Information, and Optional Information about Customers are referred to collectively as “Customer Data”.

How TuringSense Collects Your Information; Tracking and Cookies

When you register for an account with TuringSense, we may ask you to provide us with certain information in order to create your account, and to provide you with our services. This information is collected by us when you fill out forms that may be provided to you through the Service.

TuringSense, and third parties we interact with, including our third-party service providers, may use cookies, web beacons, locally shared objects (sometimes called "flash cookies"), and similar technologies in connection with your use of the Service to provide and support the Service (collectively referred to in this policy as "Cookies"). Cookies are small data files that may have unique identifiers, and reside, among other places, on your mobile device, in emails we send to you, and on our applications. Locally shared objects, or “flash cookies,” are data files that can be created on your computer by the websites you visit and are a way for websites to store information for later use. Locally stored objects are different than cookies because they are stored in different parts of your computer than cookies. Web beacons are small strings of code that provide a method for delivering a graphic image on a web page or in an email message for the purpose of transferring data.

If Cookies are used, they may be used to collect information about you and your use of the Service, such as your browser type, preferences, data relating to content that has been displayed to you or that you have clicked on, and the date and time of your use. Cookies may also be used in order to further features and processes on the Service, provide authentication and security for your transactions using the Service, store your preferences, facilitate relevant advertising, and help us learn more about how users engage with the Service.

We sometimes use service providers to help us provide certain products and services or to integrate other features. These third-party service providers may collect information when you view or use them, including information about you and your device or browser and about your online activities over time and across different websites. They may do this using Cookies or similar technologies. These third-party service providers also may use Cookies or similar technologies to help share information with us, like how you use their website or application. To learn more about the information they collect or receive, review their privacy policies.
